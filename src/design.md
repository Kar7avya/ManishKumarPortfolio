# Executive Portfolio Website â€“ Design Brief

Design a high-end, executive-level personal portfolio website for a senior industrial leader with over 30 years of experience across power generation, oil & gas, fertilizers, and large-scale infrastructure operations.

## Objective
The website must immediately communicate credibility, authority, and leadership.  
This is not a creative or student portfolio. It should feel appropriate for CXOs, board members, enterprise recruiters, and global energy organizations.

## Target Audience
- Power & energy sector executives  
- Infrastructure and EPC leadership  
- Enterprise recruiters for AVP / VP / Director roles  
- Strategic partners and consultants  

## Brand Positioning
- Calm, confident, and authoritative  
- Sophisticated and premium, not flashy  
- Industrial, strategic, trust-driven  
- Focus on scale, responsibility, and long-term impact  

## Visual Style
- Dark executive color palette (deep blue, slate, charcoal)  
- Subtle gradients only where they enhance hierarchy  
- Minimal animation, smooth and professional  
- Glassmorphism used sparingly for premium sections  
- Large typography with clear hierarchy and generous spacing  
- Mobile-first, desktop-optimized for executive viewing  

## Layout & Sections

### Hero Section
- Full-screen introduction  
- Professional portrait  
- Name and senior leadership title  
- One-line value statement emphasizing leadership and scale  

### About / Leadership Summary
- Executive biography  
- Emphasis on decision-making, responsibility, and operational scale  
- Formal and professional tone  

### Experience Timeline
- Vertical chronological timeline  
- Highlight:
  - Power plant capacities (MW)
  - Commissioning leadership
  - Operations & Maintenance responsibility
  - Organizational and team scale  

### Expertise & Competencies
- Operations leadership  
- Commissioning and maintenance  
- Safety and compliance  
- Cross-functional team leadership  
- Strategic and operational planning  

### Education
- Formal academic presentation  
- Clear and concise  

### Achievements & Impact
- Quantified outcomes  
- Capacity handled, years of experience, industries served  

### Contact & Professional Presence
- Email and phone visibility  
- Strong LinkedIn presence  
- Formal contact form for professional inquiries  

## UX Principles
- No clutter, no gimmicks  
- Clear reading flow  
- Respect executive attention span  
- Design suitable for boardroom-level presentation  

## Technical Expectations
- React-based component structure  
- Clean and readable layout  
- Performance-conscious design  
- Inline styles acceptable if intentional and consistent  

## Tone
- Professional  
- Confident  
- Strategic  
- Senior-level  

The final design should feel suitable for executive leadership and enterprise environments, not a startup demo or student project.
